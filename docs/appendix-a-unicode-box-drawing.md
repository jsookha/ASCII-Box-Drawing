## Introduction

The Unicode block **U+2500‚ÄìU+257F** contains the complete set of box‚Äëdrawing characters. These symbols were designed to allow text‚Äëbased terminals to render structured diagrams without graphics.  

Understanding the full set is useful for:

- Matching corners and edges when designing complex diagrams  
- Teaching students how different families of characters interrelate  
- Building programmatic generators that can switch styles dynamically  
- Ensuring compatibility across platforms and fonts  

This appendix groups the characters into families for easier navigation.  
Where relevant, related blocks (shading and geometric shapes) are also included.

## Teaching Notes

- **Grouping matters:** Students often confuse light vs heavy vs double lines. Presenting them side‚Äëby‚Äëside clarifies usage.  
- **Mixed joints:** These are advanced but powerful for emphasizing headers or borders. Encourage experimentation.  
- **Arc corners and diagonals:** Useful for softer UI mockups or flow diagrams.  
- **Block elements:** Ideal for progress bars, shading, or simple bar charts.  
- **Geometric shapes:** Extend the visual vocabulary beyond boxes, enabling richer diagrams.
